# Fearing PO3 / QT / MMXM Rulebook

Research basis: 45 long-form uploads, 329 Shorts, 38 timestamped long-form transcripts,
and 105 timestamped Shorts transcripts from the public Fearing YouTube channel.

This document records public, repeated rules that can be converted into deterministic chart
logic. It does not attempt to recreate private Quarterly Theory material that the creator says
is intentionally omitted from public videos.

## System Hierarchy

1. **MMXM provides price narrative.** Determine the higher-timeframe market-maker model,
   its stage, and the external draw on liquidity.
2. **PO3 provides time structure.** Frame yearly, quarterly, monthly, weekly, daily, 4-hour,
   and 30-minute candles as open-high-low-close or open-low-high-close delivery.
3. **Quarterly Theory refines timing.** Divide active time ranges into quarters and monitor
   their opens, highs, lows, failed auctions, and nested alignment.
4. **Liquidity and PD arrays provide location.** Manipulation should reach meaningful old
   highs/lows, session liquidity, imbalances, order blocks, breakers, or other arrays.
5. **SMT validates the manipulation.** SMT is confirmation for an already anticipated idea,
   not an independent entry signal.
6. **CISD provides execution.** Once manipulation and SMT are present, a body-close change
   in delivery triggers entry; a perfect FVG retracement is not mandatory.
7. **Distribution provides targets.** Target opposing liquidity, rebalancing objectives,
   range equilibrium/OT levels, and the higher-timeframe draw.

## AMD Versus PO3

- AMD is a price pattern: accumulation, manipulation, distribution.
- PO3 is a time-based candle/range framework: OLHC for bullish delivery and OHLC for bearish
  delivery.
- Every clean AMD can be framed as a PO3, but every PO3 does not need to print a textbook
  consolidation-shaped AMD.
- The same structure is fractal. A weekly PO3 contains daily PO3s; a daily PO3 contains 4-hour
  and 30-minute PO3s; distribution retracements can contain lower-timeframe AMDs.
- Lower-timeframe PO3 trades should generally continue the active higher-timeframe PO3 unless
  a validated higher-timeframe reversal is present.

## Narrative Engine

### Top-Down Order

Review active PO3 delivery from largest to smallest:

1. Yearly
2. Quarterly
3. Monthly
4. Weekly
5. Daily
6. 4-hour
7. 30-minute execution cycle

The larger cycles establish directional priority. A lower cycle can retrace against them, but
continuation setups receive higher probability when multiple cycles distribute in the same
direction.

### Market-Maker Model

- Treat MMXM as the price map and PO3 as the time map.
- Identify whether price is in original consolidation, first/second-stage reaccumulation or
  redistribution, smart-money reversal, or completed expansion.
- Each MMXM stage can be represented by a lower-timeframe PO3.
- The model's external liquidity objective supplies the directional draw.
- A bullish higher-timeframe model can contain lower-timeframe sell models during retracement,
  and vice versa.

### Trend Conditions

- In strong trend/all-time-high conditions, shallow manipulation into a valid higher-timeframe
  array plus SMT may be sufficient. Deep discount/premium retracements are not always required.
- A wickless or weakly manipulated candle (called a devil's mark in the videos) matters only
  when its direction agrees with the larger narrative.
- Missing manipulation can remain an engineered future draw; it does not provide a precise date
  for the retracement.

## PO3 Rules

### Bullish PO3 (OLHC)

1. Establish a bullish external draw.
2. Record the cycle opening price.
3. Prefer price to trade below the open into sell-side liquidity or a bullish PD array.
4. Validate the manipulation with time-aligned SMT.
5. Confirm the change to bullish delivery with CISD/displacement.
6. Distribute toward the accumulation high and external buy-side draw.

### Bearish PO3 (OHLC)

1. Establish a bearish external draw.
2. Record the cycle opening price.
3. Prefer price to trade above the open into buy-side liquidity or a bearish PD array.
4. Validate the manipulation with time-aligned SMT.
5. Confirm the change to bearish delivery with CISD/displacement.
6. Distribute toward the accumulation low and external sell-side draw.

### Manipulation Requirements

- Manipulation should interact with meaningful liquidity or a higher-timeframe PD array.
- Turtle soup, inducement, SMT, and stop raids commonly identify the manipulation.
- SMT at the manipulation extreme confirms its end in Fearing's model.
- A move without relevant SMT is not treated as fully validated manipulation.
- The manipulation can be shallow in a strong trend; location and narrative matter more than a
  fixed retracement depth.

### Distribution Confirmation

- CISD or market-structure displacement confirms that distribution has begun.
- Correct trades should generally displace away promptly after CISD. Failure to displace and
  prolonged accumulation after entry is a warning and can justify reducing risk or moving to
  break-even.

## Public Time Models

### Daily PO3

- Midnight New York is the key daily opening anchor in the public PO3 material.
- Classic bullish profile: Asia accumulation, London manipulation, New York distribution.
- Classic bearish profile is the inverse.
- London may begin distribution early; New York can then provide continuation rather than the
  original reversal.

### 30-Minute PO3

- The 9:30 a.m. New York open begins a new 30-minute PO3 and is the primary execution cycle in
  many Fearing trades.
- The cycle is interpreted relative to higher-timeframe narrative, not as an automatic reversal.
- A bullish model seeks manipulation below the 9:30 open/accumulation low, SMT, then bullish
  CISD. A bearish model seeks the inverse.
- The first valid CISD after the manipulation is favored over repeatedly entering every lower-
  timeframe signal.
- Market-open data highs/lows and significant news candles can serve as manipulation objectives.

### 4-Hour PO3

- Public examples repeatedly frame 4-hour candles beginning at significant 4-hour opens,
  including the 10:00 a.m. New York cycle.
- The 10:00 move is conditional on daily/4-hour structure. It is not simply "always reverse at
  10:00."
- When 9:30 displacement establishes a valid model, 10:00 manipulation into that model can offer
  continuation.

### Weekly PO3

- Weekly bullish delivery generally seeks manipulation below the weekly open or a prior low
  before expansion higher; bearish delivery is inverse.
- The low/high of week is not considered validated manipulation without SMT or meaningful
  liquidity interaction.
- Weekly direction is refined through each daily PO3 rather than traded as a single static bias.

## Quarterly Theory: Publicly Verifiable Layer

- Quarterly Theory is used to refine time and execution inside PO3/MMXM narrative.
- Active ranges are treated as nested quarters. Each quarter has an opening price and local
  highs/lows that can become manipulation or distribution references.
- Quarter alignment adds conviction when a lower-timeframe quarter's expected delivery agrees
  with daily/4-hour/higher-timeframe PO3.
- Fearing publicly references yearly, quarterly, monthly, weekly, daily, two-day, 4-hour, and
  30-minute opening anchors, but repeatedly withholds parts of his proprietary QT mappings.
- Therefore the code should expose deterministic quarter segmentation and opening anchors while
  labeling proprietary/undefined QT criteria as unavailable rather than inventing them.

## SMT Engine

- Use correlated assets on the same timeframe. For indices, the public model compares NQ, ES,
  and YM.
- Bullish SMT: one market raids a meaningful low while one or both correlated markets hold.
- Bearish SMT: one market raids a meaningful high while one or both correlated markets hold.
- SMT is a confirmation tool for a pre-existing narrative/location idea.
- Meaningful reference points are time-aligned: previous week, previous day, previous 4-hour,
  previous 90-minute, previous 30-minute, session highs/lows, and accumulation boundaries.
- Previous means the immediately preceding aligned range, not an arbitrary older swing.
- Stacked SMT across higher, middle, and lower timeframes increases probability.
- The market that fails to raid can offer a tighter-risk entry, but NQ is often traded regardless
  of which index creates the failure swing.

Source: [Introduction To SMT Divergence](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NZpEvXdlfQw)

## CISD Execution

- Bullish CISD: price closes above the opening price of the first candle in the most recent single
  or consecutive down-close manipulation sequence.
- Bearish CISD: price closes below the opening price of the first candle in the most recent single
  or consecutive up-close manipulation sequence.
- CISD uses candle bodies/opening prices, unlike a wick-based market-structure break.
- Closure is required; a wick through the level is not confirmation.
- Fearing's public model enters on confirmation rather than requiring a retest.
- Stop is placed beyond the manipulation extreme.
- A retest can create a CISD order block and secondary lower-timeframe confirmation.
- CISD should follow higher-timeframe SMT/location. Standalone CISDs are not enough.
- FVGs and 2022/unicorn models can add confluence but are not mandatory because price may never
  retrace into them.

Source: [This ICT Change In State Of Delivery (CISD)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cvLKUlP_SrY)

## OTE, Premium/Discount, and Repricing

- Premium/discount is a location filter, not an absolute requirement during strong trend.
- OTE remains useful for repricing and target logic. The working range is 0.62-0.79, with 0.705
  as the core level.
- Fearing's reviews also use EQ and OT levels as management/target references.
- OTE should not delay an otherwise complete SMT + CISD execution if the model expects immediate
  distribution.
- When OTE overlaps an imbalance/order block and agrees with active PO3 direction, it becomes a
  high-value continuation or secondary-entry zone.

## Liquidity and Rebalancing

Track:

- Previous day/week/month highs and lows
- Session and accumulation highs/lows
- Previous aligned 30m/90m/4h highs and lows
- Data/news candle highs and lows
- Equal highs/lows and old swing liquidity
- Gaps, FVGs, liquidity voids, and missing buy-side/sell-side delivery
- Order blocks, breakers, mitigation blocks, and CISD body zones

Rebalancing narrative matters when price has delivered aggressively in only one direction and
left a gap/void without opposing delivery.

## Entry Checklist

### Required

1. Higher-timeframe draw/narrative
2. Active PO3 cycle and opening price
3. Manipulation on the correct side of the open or accumulation range
4. Meaningful liquidity/PD-array interaction
5. Time-aligned SMT
6. Body-close CISD
7. Acceptable stop beyond the manipulation extreme
8. Clean opposing liquidity target

### Optional Confluence

- Multiple aligned higher-timeframe PO3s
- Stacked SMT across multiple timeframes
- 9:30 30m or 10:00 4h timing
- FVG/order-block/OTE overlap
- Two-day, daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, or yearly open alignment
- Data high/low interaction
- Devil's-mark continuation aligned with narrative

## Management

- Primary invalidation is beyond the manipulation extreme.
- Internal liquidity or range equilibrium can be used for first reduction/break-even decisions.
- External opposing liquidity is the primary distribution target.
- OTE/OT and unmitigated arrays can be intermediate targets.
- Do not move to break-even solely because price moved slightly. Use a logical internal objective
  or confirmed distribution.
- Failure to displace promptly after CISD is a warning.

## Deterministic Implementation Boundary

The Pine system can reliably automate:

- Nested opening anchors and time quarters
- PO3 direction/state around each open
- 9:30 30m and 10:00 4h cycles
- Previous aligned liquidity levels
- Triad-style SMT using two comparison symbols
- Body-close CISD and CISD order-block zones
- OTE/equilibrium projections
- FVG/order-block overlap
- Top-down alignment score
- Entry, invalidation, targets, and alerts

The Pine system cannot honestly automate without additional definitions/data:

- Private Quarterly Theory mappings not stated publicly
- Discretionary MMXM stage labeling with perfect human accuracy
- Unscheduled news interpretation from Financial Juice
- Astrology claims beyond simple calendar/lunar tags
- Guaranteed profitability or live execution quality
